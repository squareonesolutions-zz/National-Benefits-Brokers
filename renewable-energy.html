Take control of your energy cost

Creating a hedge against the volatility of traditional energy rates is one of the many benefits of solar power — you'll immediately see profitable results, and you'll continue to see them in the face of changes in the energy economy. Increased home value

In today's market, energy efficiency measures and renewable electricity generation adds value to a home. Recent surveys show that every $1,000 saved per year on electricity costs adds $20,000 to a home's value. Solar energy is therefore one of the best home improvement investments you make.

What finance options exist to enable Households and Firms to convert to solar power?

A range of financing options are available, from funding the system outright to making no upfront investment at all via a power purchase agreement. Specialized leasing and debt options, as well as hybrid arrangements, are also available. The costs and benefits of the various approaches vary substantially and no single solution is appropriate to the needs of all businesses. In general, however, the range of alternatives available is such that financing issues are rarely an impediment for firms converting to solar electricity. Ask us about Sun Run for your residential
home today.

Actual Cost of Installation

With a combined 65% of your system cost covered by tax credits and other incentives, a solar installation is surprisingly affordable. Add to that the fact that once your solar installation is paid for, your electricity costs will be drastically reduced (if not disappear completely!). We offer a number of financing options to make your investment as smart as it can be.

Why Solar Power for your business? How about a possible 3-year ROI.

Hawaii has the nation's highest electric rates, and with the vast majority of Hawaii's power coming from shipped-in petroleum, they're not going to get any lower. With skyrocketing utility bills and the available state and federal tax incentives, a $1,000,000 investment in a solar photovoltaic system will pay for itself in about three years on Oahu. On neighboring islands, return on investment is even faster due to higher electricity rates.
