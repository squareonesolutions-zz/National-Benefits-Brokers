What we offer:

We assist your student for all undergraduate years with: board exam test preparation, choosing a major, choosing a college or university, financial aid negotiations, FAFSA / CSS Profile filing, and maximizing your financial aid eligibility to get the most grants and scholarships allowable.

Why are Guidance Counselors and Financial Aid Officers Not Enough?

National Benefit Brokers certified Counselors fill the gaps where your guidance counselors and financial aid officers can’t possibly help. Why, because they are not licensed financial professionals who understand the financial aid system through and through. Our allegiance is with your family. Financial aid officers work for the schools. Their job is to get as much money for their school as possible. Colleges and universities are businesses. Sitting down with a financial aid officer to complete your FAFSA or CSS Profile is exactly like going to the local IRS office to complete your income tax returns. They might help you complete the forms. The chances of you being in the best situation possible are slim to none.

The vast majority of high school guidance counselors usually assist with: certain aspects of admissions, obtaining general information about schools, and other minor details such as filing deadlines or board exam testing dates. They are not the appropriate people to assist with matters pertaining to financial aid. Especially when it comes to FAFSA/CSS Profile filings. The guidance counselor will likely not understand how every line on the forms affects how much you’re expected to pay in college costs. Let us just say you happen to have a one-in-a-thousand, financially savvy guidance counselor who does understand the forms. The person still likely won’t be able to help you. This is because the guidance counselor is not a licensed financial professional who understands investments. Above all, the guidance counselor will not be available during the college years.

Why Your Financial Advisor May be Hazardous to Your Wealth

It is not a good idea to see your financial advisor about college planning unless your advisor understands every line on the FAFSA and CSS Profile forms. The financial advisor can’t possibly put you in the proper investments without such expertise. 123 College spends hundreds of thousands of dollars obtaining and updating the data necessary for you to make sound college planning decisions. Please make sure any financial professional can clearly explain each line of the FAFSA or CSS Profile before taking any of their college planning advice.
